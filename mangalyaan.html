Indian mission to mars

The Mars Orbiter Mission, fondly called as MOM is India's first venture into interplanetary space. It's main objective is to study Mars surface features, morphology, mineralogy and the Martian atmosphere. MOM is also expected to explore the existence or possiblity of life on Mars by searching for meathne in Martian atmosphere.

The huge distances in interplanetary travel is a daunting task in itself. It involves developing and mastering complex technologies. But this endeavour opens up never-ending opportunities for future space explorataion. After the launch from the Earth, the orbiter had to endure the harshest of environment during its 300 day long journey before attaining Martian orbit. To handle unexpected situations during its journey, the Mars orbiter has a lot of autonomy except in some areas of deep space communications and navigation-guidance-control capabilities.

Once India announced its Mars ambition, ISRO quickly stepped up to the task as the nearest launch window was only a few months away. If missed this window, ISRO would have had to wait another 780 days for launching.Hence, the important tasks such as mission planning, manufacturing of the launch vehicle and the spacecraft and preparing the support systems had to take place very quickly
